MISSION STATEMENT

The mission of the Miami University Sports Medicine Department is to create and maintain an environment which will provide: (a) an avenue for sports medicine practitioners to successfully provide the care, prevention and rehabilitation of athletic injuries and (b) the opportunity for student athletic trainers to become competent in the theories, techniques and practices of Athletic Training. To achieve this, several areas will need to be addressed.

1. Each staff member must constantly strive to stay abreast of the changes in Sports Medicine so that the quality of care will continue to improve. This will require continued acquisition of clinical and academic knowledge through meetings, seminars, clinics and information derived from the information network from within the profession.

2. Each Sports Medicine staff member must look at the tasks to be done and determine what they are uniquely qualified to do and what can be delegated to another member of the staff. At no time should a staff member delegate a task to a person who is not uniquely qualified for such a task. The staff must recognize that their decisions not only affect them but will affect the rest of the staff as well. Therefore, it is important that each staff member look at their tasks as vitally important and carry them out or request support from the rest of the staff. A decision can then be made by the Head Athletic Trainer and the Director of Sports Medicine as to the priority of the tasks. We must work to create an environment in which student athletic trainers are competent and self confident enough to carry out some of the other duties which would take us away from that which only we are qualified to do and to provide the student-athlete trainers the avenues by which they may achieve certification in the NATA and employment in athletic training.

 

INTRODUCTION

The Miami University Sports Medicine Department provides for the prevention, treatment and rehabilitation of athletic injuries under the direction of our team physicians and in particular the Director of Sports Medicine. The Yager Stadium training room serves field hockey, softball, men's & women's soccer, men's and women's track and field, men's and women's cross country and football. The Withrow training room serves wrestling, baseball, men's and women's swimming/diving, men's & women's tennis and golf. The Millett training room serves men's and women's basketball and volleyball. The Goggin Ice Arena training room serves ice hockey and synchronized skating. Specific hours for each facility are posted at the entrance doors. Although each training room serves specific sports it should be noted that any athlete is welcome in any of the training facilities. The Withrow Court training room is also the location where morning treatments are held unless other arrangements are made.

 

PHYSICAL EXAMINATION

A. New student athletes: All student athletes participating at Miami University for the first time shall undergo a physical examination and be cleared to participate by the Director of Sports Medicine. In addition, all forms required by the Sports Medicine Office; including the health appraisal, medical history, family history and the insurance questionnaire must be completed and on file prior to the first practice.

B. Returning student athletes: All returning student athletes shall have on file the current health appraisal and insurance forms required by the Sports Medicine Office prior to the first practice.

C. Open tryouts: All student athletes participating in an open try-out must have a written statement from their physician stating that they have been examined within the past 12 months and have been found physically fit to participate in Intercollegiate Athletics with no restrictions. This note plus a health appraisal must be on file with the Sports Medicine Office prior to the first practice. Once these student athletes have been picked for the team they must complete all requirements under category A. (New student athletes).

 

PHYSICIAN REFERRALS

Referrals of student athletes to one of the team doctors will be made by one of the certified athletic trainers. This will be done by having an injury evaluation sheet completed and present when the athlete is seen by the physician. The athlete's name will be added to the list of appointments for the doctor to see and kept in the training room. The physician or their designee can make the appropriate notation on the injury evaluation sheet which will be added to the athlete's file. Any test results, x-rays or other information that may help the physician make a diagnosis should also be present at the time the student athlete is seen. A list will be kept in the training facility where the doctors will hold their clinic and those athletes that are to be seen by the doctor will have their names on this list. Those athletes that fail to report for the doctor's clinic will be reported to their respective coaches.

Any referral of a student athlete to a physician outside of our team physicians will be made by the Director of Sports Medicine who will fill out a referral form and a Certified Athletic Trainer will make the appointment.

 

MEDICATION

Athletically Related Injury or Illness

The Athletic Department will be responsible for therapeutic drugs required as a direct result of athletic injury or illness related to athletic activity and approved by one of our team physicians. The team physician will write the medication prescription and the attending staff athletic trainer will fill out and initial the red permission card for charging this medication to the Athletic Department at the Student Health Service. In the event that the Student Health Service is closed the athlete can be either taken or sent to the Kroger Pharmacy and receive their medication using an Athletic Department charge card.

Any prescription medications that the team physicians wish to have present in the training room will be stored in a locked cabinet, drawer or other secure receptacle, and will be used as a starter dosage in compliance with Ohio law.

 

Non Athletically Related Injury or Illness

The Athletic Department will not be responsible for medication expenses for non athletic related injury or illness. The athletes will still be seen by one of our team physicians who may write a prescription for medication, but responsibility for payment lies with the athlete.

 

Non Prescription Medications (Over the Counter)

Non prescription medications that are kept in the training rooms, including but not limited to aspirin, non-aspirin pain relievers, ibuprofen, anti-diarrheals, shall be kept in an area of the training room that is accessible to all the athletic training staff members but not the student-athletes. This will enable the sports medicine staff to regulate the amount and frequency of a particular medication that a student athlete is receiving.

 

REHABILITATION ROOM

The rehabilitation room located in Yager Stadium is operated and staffed by Oxford Physical Therapy and Rehabilitation. Athletes who are injured and require rehabilitation will be referred to Oxford Physical Therapy and Rehabilitation by the Director of Sports Medicine for evaluation and initial therapy. At such time as the staff at OPTR feels it appropriate, rehabilitation will be continued in the Yager Stadium rehabilitation room. In most cases the visits to OPTR will be kept to a minimum, usually 1-4 times, but may be more in number depending on the injury and length of rehabilitation. Athletes will be billed for visits to OPTR. but not to the Yager Stadium rehabilitation room.

 

MEDICAL RECORDS

1. Each staff athletic trainer is responsible for generating and maintaining current files on each of the student athletes participating in programs under their care. Completed medical files would include.

a. a complete medical history of the student athlete
b. a family health history
c. an insurance information sheet
d. a health appraisal
e. a physical form signed by the Director of Sports Medicine

Each summer a mailing will be sent to the parents of each athlete. Those athletes who are coming to Miami for the first time will be sent forms A - B - C - and D. Those athletes who are returning to Miami will be sent forms C and D. These forms must be on file in the Sports Medicine Office prior to the athlete's arrival on campus.

2. Each injury will be documented on an injury evaluation sheet.

3. Each treatment will be documented on the injury evaluation sheet.

4. Each physician appointment and dictation should be documented on the injury evaluation sheet.

5. Any referrals to physicians outside of our team physician will be documented on the injury evaluation sheet.

6. When an athlete has recovered and does not require any more treatment for an injury the injury evaluation sheet will be placed in the athlete's medical file.

 

Insurance Coverage and Procedures

A. The NCAA does not permit Miami University or any college or university to provide coverage or pay the bills incurred for injury, illness or conditions which are not a direct result of participation in our intercollegiate sport program.

B. Miami University requires every student to be covered by a primary insurance carrier whether this be a personal policy, coverage under a parent's policy, coverage under the Miami University Student Health Insurance policy or a combination of these policies.

C. An insurance questionnaire will be sent to each student athlete during the summer along with an explanation of our athletic insurance coverage. Those athletes who fail to return this information will not be allowed to participate until this information is on file in the Sports Medicine Office. (Physical Examinations A & B).

D. The Athletic Insurance Policy provides coverage for student athletes injured while participating in the play or practice of an intercollegiate sport.

E. The Athletic Insurance Policy provides secondary coverage only. If at the time of injury the student athlete is not covered by a primary insurance policy they will be held responsible for the deductible on the Athletic Insurance Policy which is currently $2000.00.

F. The Athletic Insurance Policy cannot provide benefits for pre-existing conditions. The decision on whether a condition was pre-existing will be made by the Director of Sports Medicine.

G. Claims will be handled by full time staff athletic trainers (See Specific Administrative Duties).

H. Proper documentation in the form of itemized bills and EOBs (explanation of benefits) is required before Miami University will play any part of a medical bill. The student athlete is responsible for providing this documentation.

 

Non-Athletic Related Injury

Any student athlete who is injured outside of intercollegiate athletics may still be seen by the Sports Medicine Staff and Team Physicians. However, any expenses which are generated are the responsibility of the student athlete.

 

Optical Care

1. The Athletic Department will purchase contact lenses or glasses for any athlete who requires them for safe/productive participation in intercollegiate athletes.

2. Athletes who are in need of glasses or contacts will be referred to an eye specialist by one of the staff athletic trainers. The doctor will do the initial examination and fitting of the glasses or contacts.

3. Purchase of lenses and glasses shall be limited to the basic requirement for safe participation as judged by the doctor. Any additional costs for special frames or lenses will be the responsibility of the athlete.

4. The Athletic Department will purchase replacement contacts or glasses when lost or damaged as a direct result of athletic participation.

5. Bills for the eye examination and lenses or glasses will go first to the athletes primary insurance and the balance to be paid by the Athletic Department.

 

Dental Care

1. The athletic department will only be responsible for dental injury that is a direct result of an accident that occurs during a regularly scheduled practice or contest.

2. The athletic department will only be responsible for dental injury that is a direct result of an accident that occurs while wearing an intra oral device covering, at least, all upper teeth if this is a requirement according to NCAA rules.

3. The athletic department will only be responsible for dental injury to sound and natural teeth.

4. Athletes who receive a dental injury will be referred to a dentist by one of the staff trainers. The dentist will do the initial examination, treatment and arrange for follow up as needed or referral to another specialist.

5. A dental injury occurring to a student athlete that does not fall into categories 1, 2, or 3 will still be handled as indicated in #4, but the student athlete will be responsible for all bills associated with this injury.

 

HOT WEATHER POLICY

The occasion for serious heat problems does exist in Oxford, OH. To insure the safety of our student athletes the following guidelines have been established.

1. During times of hot and humid weather, a member of the sports medicine staff will determine the air temperature and relative humidity using a sling psychrometer or the weather service. Coaches that will not have a trainer present at practice should be informed of these conditions. (i.e. Cross Country training off campus or any training or conditioning programs held away from campus.)

2. The following chart will be used as guidelines for practice in hot and humid conditions.

Temp F      Humidity      Procedure
80 - 90 Under 70% Watch those athletes that tend toward obesity.
80 - 90 & Over 70% Athletes should take a 10 min. rest every hour and tee shirts should be changed when wet. All athletes should be under constant and careful supervision.
90 - 100 Under 70%

over 100		 Over 70% Under these conditions it would be well to suspend practice. A shortened program conducted in shorts and tee shirts could be established.


OR
Using the wet bulb temp only

WBT F      Recommendations
60 & below No prevention necessary.
61 -65 Alert persons to symptoms of heat stress and the importance of adequate hydration.
66 - 70 Insist that adequate water be ingested.
71 -75 Rest periods and water breaks every 20-30 minutes; place limits on intense activity.
76 - 79 Modify practice considerably and curtail activity for unacclimated individuals.
80 & above Cancel practice.


3. The above guidelines should be used to decide when practice should be altered or cancelled. The athletic trainer in charge of the sport should take into account the conditioning level and acclimatization level of the athletes when making a decision on cancelling or altering practice or contests. The athletic trainer will make the final decision (recommendation) on canceling or altering practice.

4. Student athletes that participate in sports where heat and humidity are of greatest concern: football, soccer, cross country, field hockey and wrestling will be required to weigh in and weigh out at practices and contests. Losses of 3-5 % of body weight after practice indicates dehydration. A return weight before the next practice should be within 1-2 % of their original weight. Athletes with a 3-4 % loss should be restricted in practice and those with a 5 % or greater weight loss between practices should be held out of all activity and report to the Director of Sports Medicine.

5. Sports that utilize conditioning programs away from their normal facility (such as distance runs) present a problem and coaches should make provisions for supervision and the availability of fluids for these athletes along the course.

 

COMMUNICABLE SKIN PROBLEMS

In order to reduce the risk of communicable skin diseases with our student wrestlers the following protocols shall be followed.

1. During the wrestling season the mats in the wrestling room will be cleaned by the building custodial staff before and after each practice.

2. The locker room and showers shall be cleaned and disinfected on a daily basis by the building custodial staff and inspected by the head wrestling coach and supervising athletic trainer on a weekly basis.

3. The showers shall be supplied with anti-microbial soap by the building custodial staff.

4. Kern-Shield (or a similar product) will be provided to the wrestlers for application prior to practice to protect themselves against communicable diseases.

5. Wrestling shoes and head gear shall be placed on the top of the locker, sprayed with Q-Zene or similar solution and, allowed to air dry before the next practice. (The solution of Q-Zene will be provided by the training staff.)

6. Each wrestler will be issued two sets of practice gear and shall be laundered by the equipment staff and returned prior to each practice. Any athlete wearing personal equipment, sweats, gloves etc. must make sure that this equipment is laundered prior to each practice.

7. A solution of Q-Zene (or similar solution) will be made available in the wrestling room by the trainer for cleaning the exercise bikes and equipment following each use.

8. A list of athletes with a history of skin infections will be given to the Strength and Conditioning Coordinator (Dan Dairymple) and shall be updated daily so that special cleaning of the weight room equipment can take place following those athletes' workouts.

9. The training room personnel will take the following added precaution when dealing with people with histories of or suspicion of skin infections.

(a) Do not re-use ice bags.
(b) Hot pack covers laundered prior to use by another athlete.
(c) Tables cleaned prior to use by another athlete.
(d) Modality pads cleaned prior to use by another athlete.
(e) Whirlpools cleaned prior to use by another athlete.

In addition, all athletes with a history of skin susceptible conditions will be evaluated by Dr. Dailey on a yearly basis (prior to the season's start) and placed on medicine prophylacticly if deemed appropriate.

Any student athlete who reports a skin condition must be evaluated by a physician prior to practice. The wrestling trainer in conjunction with the team physician will make a determination as to the practice status of the athlete.

 

EMERGENCY PLAN

The Sports Medicine staff at Miami University is committed to quality care in the treatment of athletic injuries and emergency situations. The following guidelines will be followed in emergency situations.

Conditions that may warrant emergency care.

  1. Heat illness
  2. Head injury
  3. Neck or spine injury
  4. Respiratory difficulties
  5. Fractures, dislocations
  6. Severe bleeding
  7. Shock

Individuals making the decision

  1. Team Physician
  2. Certified Athletic Trainer
  3. Student Athletic Trainer
  4. Coaches (when none of the above are present)

 

Activation of EMS

1. Situation identified as an emergency situation.

2. Medical person in charge stays with the athlete to administer first-aid and sends someone else to call 911 (Miami University Police).

3. What to say when calling 911             Dial 911

Dispatcher: Miami 911 What is your emergency?
Caller: We have an emergency situation at ____________(exact place) and the emergency is ___________(be specific).
Dispatcher: Please hang on the line while I dispatch officers.

The dispatcher at this time will request more information about the emergency situation and ask if the life squad should also be dispatched. (They may automatically dispatch the life squad at the same time they dispatch the officers depending on the emergency.

4. The person making the call should wait for the Miami University Police at the designated entrance and direct or take them to the emergency situation area, then return to meet the life squad (if they have been dispatched) and take them to the emergency situation area.

5. The medical person in charge will make the decision as to who goes with the athlete to the hospital.

     

VACATION POLICY

Recognizing that athletes require year round attention to their medical needs and that administrative tasks must be accomplished in a timely fashion, the Sports Medicine department has established the following policy to assure that all staff has adequate time off while continuing to function on a 12 month calendar.

1. School year - Vacation time can be taken during the academic year if the staff trainer's teams are not practicing and in the judgement of the Head Athletic Trainer quality care can be provided to all athletes in the staff member's absence.

2. Summer - The staff is encouraged to take vacation during this period. Requests will be reviewed and approved by the Head Athletic Trainer under the following guidelines.

    a. Adequate staffing of the facilities for summer treatment must be maintained.
    b. Continued communication about any ongoing problems that they have regardless of their vacation status. This means that the staff trainer on vacation must either communicate appropriately to staff on duty or communicate directly to the athlete and coach via phone in their absence.

3. In general, staff are expected to be gone no more than two consecutive weeks. This will insure that the staff member has a chance to check the status of his or her athletes and to get caught up on administrative duties.

4. All staff are required to submit vacation leave requests with the athletic department with a copy of this request to the Head Athletic Trainer.

5. Professional development time will not be counted against vacation time provided that the Head Athletic Trainer and/or the Director of Sports Medicine is satisfied that the activity will enhance the sports medicine program.
